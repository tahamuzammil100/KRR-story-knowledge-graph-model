1. Overview & Architecture

The ontology is split into seven modules plus the instance file. Modules import each other in a directed acyclic graph:

rcc.ttl (given)    fo.ttl (given)    meo.ttl (given, imports fo)
     |                   |                  |
     v                   v                  v
place.ttl          social.ttl          time.ttl
      \                  |              /
       \                 v             /
        +---------> event.ttl <-------+
                        |
                   action.ttl
                        |
                    plot.ttl
                        |
               narn_instances.ttl (imports all)

2. Place Module place.ttl

Imports rcc:. Defines a hierarchy of places and spatial relations.

Classes

TermLabelDescription
npo:PlaceplaceA named location. Subclass of rcc:Region, so all RCC8 relations apply.
npo:SettlementsettlementAn inhabited place (city, village, stronghold). Disjoint with WildArea.
npo:WildAreawild areaAn uninhabited stretch of land. Disjoint with Settlement.
npo:FortressfortressA fortified settlement built for military defence.
npo:CitycityA large organised settlement.
npo:CaverncavernAn underground place. If inside a settlement, inferred to be a settlement too.
npo:ForestforestSubclass of WildArea.
npo:MountainmountainSubclass of WildArea.
npo:RiverriverA flowing body of water, often a natural boundary.
npo:GeographicRegiongeographic regionA large area containing multiple places. Must contain at least one subpart.

Properties

TermLabelCharacteristicsDescription
npo:isSubpartOfis subpart ofTransitive, Irreflexive; subPropertyOf rcc:ntppX is entirely inside Y with no shared boundary.
npo:hasSubparthas subpartTransitive, Irreflexive; inverse of isSubpartOfY entirely contains X.
npo:bordersbordersSymmetric; subPropertyOf rcc:ecTwo places share a boundary but do not overlap.
npo:isLocatedInis located inTransitiveGeneral containment, looser than isSubpartOf.
npo:isDisconnectedFromis disconnected fromSymmetric; subPropertyOf rcc:dcNo common part, no touching. Used in the simultaneity axiom.
npo:hasCapitalhas capital—Links a region to its principal settlement.
Key axioms:
  • isSubpartOf is transitive and irreflexive — no place is a subpart of itself.
  • Settlement and WildArea are disjoint.
  • A Cavern that isSubpartOf some Settlement is inferred to be a Settlement (e.g., Menegroth).
  • A GeographicRegion must have at least one subpart (cardinality ≥ 1 on hasSubpart).

  • owesAllegianceTo is derived via property chain isMemberOf ∘ isLordOf.

4. Time Module time.ttl

Standalone module implementing Allen's interval algebra with all 13 temporal relations.

Classes

TermLabelDescription
nti:TemporalEntitytemporal entitySuperclass. Something with a temporal position.
nti:TimeInstanttime instantA dimensionless point in time. Disjoint with TimeInterval.
nti:TimeIntervaltime intervalA duration bounded by beginning and end instants. Must have exactly 1 hasBeginning and 1 hasEnd.
nti:DurationdurationThe length of an interval, independent of position.

Allen's 13 Relations

RelationInverseDescription
nti:beforenti:afterA ends before B begins
nti:meetsnti:metByA ends exactly where B begins
nti:overlapsnti:overlappedByA starts before B and ends inside B
nti:duringnti:containsA is entirely within B
nti:startsnti:startedByA and B begin together; A ends first
nti:finishesnti:finishedByA and B end together; B starts first
nti:equalsself (symmetric)Identical extent
Key axioms:
  • All 13 Allen relations are pairwise disjoint (owl:AllDisjointProperties) — two intervals can satisfy only one.
  • before and after are irreflexive and transitive.
  • equals is symmetric and transitive.
  • All relations are sub-properties of nti:hasTemporalRelation, allowing generic temporal queries.

5. Event Module event.ttl

Imports npo:, nti:, meo:, loc:. Central module linking characters, places, and time.

Event Hierarchy

TermDescription
nev:EventRoot class. Must occur at ≥1 place.
nev:BattleArmed confrontation. Must involve ≥1 EvilBeing.
nev:WarProlonged conflict containing ≥1 Battle.
nev:JourneyTravel event. Has exactly 1 departure and 1 arrival place.
nev:MeetingEncounter between ≥2 characters at one location.
nev:BirthComing into existence. Exactly 1 subject.
nev:DeathCeasing to exist. Exactly 1 subject.
nev:ExileExpulsion or departure from home.
nev:BetrayalTrust broken between characters.
nev:CurseSupernatural doom laid on a character.
nev:CaptureCharacter taken prisoner.
nev:RescueCharacter freed from captivity.

Key Properties

TermDomain → RangeDescription
nev:occursAtEvent → PlaceWhere the event happens.
nev:involvesCharacterEvent → owl:ThingAny participant.
nev:hasSubjectEvent → owl:ThingPrimary character (subPropertyOf involvesCharacter).
nev:hasAgentEvent → owl:ThingCharacter who triggers the event.
nev:hasTemporalExtentEvent → TimeIntervalWhen the event takes place.
nev:causedByEvent → EventDirect trigger.

nev:precedesEvent → EventTransitive, Irreflexive; subPropertyOf nti:before.
nev:hasItemEvent → owl:ThingObject involved in the event.
Key axioms:
  • A Battle must involve at least one meo:EvilBeing (restriction on involvesCharacter).
  • Birth and Death each have exactly 1 subject (cardinality constraint).
  • A War must have at least one Battle as a part.
  • Simultaneity axiom: events occurring at disconnected places cannot share a character at the same time — enforced by a nested restriction using npo:isDisconnectedFrom.

6. Action Module action.ttl

Imports nev:. Actions are specialised events performed by an agent with possible intent.

Action Hierarchy

TermDescription
nac:ActionSubclass of Event. Must have ≥1 agent.
nac:IntentionalActionPerformed deliberately. Must have ≥1 purpose. Disjoint with Unintentional.
nac:UnintentionalActionAccident, reflex, or compulsion. Disjoint with Intentional.
nac:PhysicalActionInvolves bodily movement or force.
nac:ViolentActionSubclass of Physical. Must have ≥1 patient.
nac:SpeechActPerformed through language.
nac:CoerciveActionForces another agent to behave in a certain way.
nac:MentalActionDeciding, remembering, forgetting.

Items

TermDescription
nac:ItemPhysical object usable as instrument.
nac:WeaponItem for combat (e.g., Gurthang).
nac:ArtefactCrafted object with story significance.

Key Properties

TermDescription
nac:hasAgentWho performs the action (subPropertyOf involvesCharacter).
nac:hasPatientWho receives the action.
nac:hasPurposeGoal or intention (domain: IntentionalAction).
nac:hasInstrumentItem used — answers "what item was used in event E?"
nac:hasResultOutcome event or state.
Key axioms:
  • IntentionalAction ∩ ViolentAction ⊆ CoerciveAction (intersection subclass).
  • A SpeechAct ∩ IntentionalAction that hasResult some Event is a CoerciveAction (e.g., Morgoth's curse).
  • Property chain on hasResult.

7. Plot Structure Module plot.ttl

Imports nev: and nac:. Models the Freytag pyramid and narrative structure from outside the fictional world.

Classes (Freytag pyramid)

TermDescription
npl:StoryA complete narrative. Must have exactly 1 Climax and ≥1 PlotArc.
npl:ChapterNamed subdivision of a story.
npl:PlotArcThematic narrative trajectory.
npl:PlotPointA narrative turning point.
npl:ExpositionOpening. Precedes RisingAction.
npl:RisingActionBuilds tension. Precedes Climax.
npl:ClimaxPeak tension. Precedes FallingAction. Unique per story.
npl:FallingActionAfter climax. Precedes Resolution.
npl:ResolutionConclusion. Follows Climax (transitively).
npl:SubplotSecondary arc. isSubplotOf some Story.
npl:TragedyStory in which the protagonist dies. HasProtagonist some (subject of some Death).
Key axioms:
  • Every Story has exactly 1 Climax (cardinality 1 on hasClimax).
  • Freytag ordering enforced: Exposition precedes RisingAction precedes Climax precedes FallingAction precedes Resolution.
  • The five narrative stages are pairwise disjoint (AllDisjointClasses).
  • A Tragedy must have a protagonist who is the subject of some Death event.

9. Competency Questions

Q: Who is parent of Túrin?
Query: ?x fo:isAParentOf narn:turin
Answer: narn:hurin, narn:morwen
Q: Who is married to Húrin?
Query: narn:hurin fo:isMarriedTo ?x
Answer: narn:morwen
Q: Where did Túrin go during the story?
Query: ?e nev:involvesCharacter narn:turin . ?e nev:occursAt ?place
Answer: Menegroth (Doriath), Dimbar, Nargothrond, Brethil
Q: Who was involved in the Fall of Nargothrond?
Query: narn:fallOfNargothrond nev:involvesCharacter ?x
Answer: Túrin, Glaurung, Finduilas, Morgoth
Q: Did the Nirnaeth happen before the Fall of Nargothrond?
Query: narn:nirnaeth nev:precedes narn:fallOfNargothrond
Answer: Yes (transitively via intermediate events)
Q: Where did the Fall of Nargothrond occur?
Query: narn:fallOfNargothrond nev:occursAt ?place
Answer: narn:nargothrond
Q: Is it possible to be in Angband and Nargothrond at the same time?
Answer: No — narn:angband npo:isDisconnectedFrom narn:nargothrond and the simultaneity axiom in event.ttl rules this out.
Q: What event ends the Narn?
Query: narn:narnStory npl:endsWithEvent ?e
Answer: narn:turinDeath
Q: What item was used in Túrin's suicide?
Query: narn:turinSuicide nac:hasInstrument ?item
Answer: narn:gurthang (Gurthang, the black sword)

10. Design Choices

Why separate modules?

Separation of concerns makes each module independently reusable. The place module can be reused for any story; the time module for any temporal ontology; the social module for any feudal world. Imports are strictly acyclic.

Why Allen's algebra rather than W3C Time Ontology?

Allen's 13 relations are exhaustive and mutually exclusive for intervals, which allows the AllDisjointProperties axiom to enforce consistency. W3C OWL-Time is richer but more complex; Allen's algebra is sufficient to answer all competency questions about event ordering.

Why is Action a subclass of Event?

Events are the most general class of things that happen. Actions are events with an agent and optional intent. This design follows the BBC Event Ontology pattern and avoids duplication of temporal and spatial properties.

Why is Menegroth both a Cavern and a Settlement?

Menegroth is explicitly an underground city. The axiom in place.ttl infers this: a Cavern that is a subpart of a Settlement is itself a Settlement. This demonstrates OWL's open-world assumption and inference power.

Tragedy and the Fatal Flaw

The Narn is one of the great literary tragedies. Modelling npl:Tragedy as a subclass of npl:Story with its own properties (hasFatalFlaw) and axiom (protagonist must die) captures the genre-level knowledge.

11. AI Tool Usage

An AI assistant (large language model) was used as a proxy expert in several stages of this project:

Plot structure (npl:)

The assistant was consulted about the standard Freytag five-act dramatic structure and how it maps onto the Narn. It confirmed that the Fall of Nargothrond is the correct climax and that Túrin's death is the resolution.

Action ontology (nac:)

The assistant was asked about the ontological distinction between events and actions (citing Davidson's action theory). It suggested the agent/patient/purpose/instrument/result property structure, noting that intentional actions require a purpose while unintentional ones do not. The distinction between physical, speech, and mental actions was also suggested by the assistant based on philosophy of action literature.

OWL axiom design

The assistant suggested using owl:AllDisjointProperties for the Allen relations (ensuring mutual exclusivity) and the property chain isMemberOf ∘ isLordOf for deriving allegiance. The simultaneity impossibility axiom was designed with the assistant's guidance, using nested OWL restrictions over npo:isDisconnectedFrom.
